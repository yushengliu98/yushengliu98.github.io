Yusheng Liu

I’m a fourth-year PhD student at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign (UIUC) in Prof. Hua Wang’s lab, where I develop engineering strategies to tag, probe, and target the cell membrane for both fundamental biological understanding and biomedical applications.
I established the first in vivo metabolic glycan labeling platform for red blood cells, enabling long-term tracking and targeted delivery. To improve labeling efficiency in antigen presenting cells, I found that inhibiting GlcNAc 2-epimerase enhances unnatural mannosamine sugae incorporation. I also developed unnatural lipids for simutanuious mRNA delivery and cell labeling. Most recently, I validated  glycoRNA on dendritic cells surface and revealed its role in T cell activation,  suggesting ​the important role of cell-surface RNAs in cellular immunity.
Moving forward, I aim to integrate computational tools to further elucidate immune cell behavior. I am now seeking a postdoctoral opportunity to advance immune engineering and translational chemical biology.
